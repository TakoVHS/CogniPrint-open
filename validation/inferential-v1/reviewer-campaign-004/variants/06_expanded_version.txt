CogniPrint is gradually becoming a reproducible environment for empirical work on text-profile stability within a cautious research programme. In the current workflow, each campaign is organised around one baseline note together with a set of controlled variants that preserve the same topic while changing one main dimension at a time, such as punctuation, lexical choice, sentence segmentation, stylistic register, or degree of compression. The purpose of this workflow is not to make definitive judgments about source or authorship, and it is not being framed as a forensic decision system. Instead, the workstation records profile shifts, metric deltas, and campaign-level summaries that can later be reviewed by colleagues in a transparent and repeatable way. This structure helps turn intuitive impressions about textual similarity into explicit empirical notes, LaTeX-ready tables, provenance-aware dataset scaffolds, and manuscript-oriented materials for further scientific work.
