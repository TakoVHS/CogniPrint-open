# External Methodological Review Policy

## Purpose

This policy defines what may and may not be counted as an external methodological review for CogniPrint.

## Current baseline

```text
Scientific readiness: descriptive_only
External methodological reviews: 0/1
```

## What counts as a methodological review

A review may be counted only when it is a substantive external methodological assessment from a named qualified person or group.

The assessment should address at least one of the following:

- feature-map assumptions;
- perturbation and stability model;
- empirical protocol;
- benchmark provenance;
- reproducibility;
- statistical or NLP methodology risks;
- limitations and claims boundaries.

## What does not count

The following must not be counted as a methodological review:

- AI responses;
- ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Codex, or other model output;
- acknowledgements;
- auto-replies;
- generic encouragement;
- funding correspondence;
- administrative messages;
- confirmation that a message was received;
- comments that do not assess the methodology.

## Counter update rule

The counter may change from `0/1` to `1/1` only after a qualifying external methodological review is received and archived with sufficient evidence.

## Readiness update rule

Scientific readiness must remain `descriptive_only` unless a separate documented readiness review justifies a change.

A single methodological review does not automatically imply validation, accuracy, deployment readiness, authorship attribution capability, AI-origin detection capability, forensic status, or legal admissibility.

## Owner confirmation

Any update to review count or readiness requires owner review and an explicit pull request.
