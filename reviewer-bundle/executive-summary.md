# CogniPrint Reviewer Bundle — Executive Summary

## Purpose

CogniPrint is an open research framework for constructing compact, interpretable statistical profiles of text and studying profile similarity and perturbation stability.

The purpose of this reviewer bundle is to support a real external methodological review of the framework, its assumptions, its empirical protocol, and its reproducibility boundaries.

## Current public baseline

- Release: `v0.1.2`
- DOI: `10.5281/zenodo.20756421`
- Repository: `https://github.com/TakoVHS/CogniPrint-open`
- Scientific readiness: `descriptive_only`
- External methodological reviews: `0/1`

## What CogniPrint claims

CogniPrint claims only a descriptive research status. It defines a feature-map framework for text profiles, comparison metrics for those profiles, and conservative perturbation/stability statements under explicit assumptions.

## What CogniPrint does not claim

CogniPrint does not establish authorship, source, AI origin, legal status, forensic identity, or a universal classification result.

CogniPrint does not claim external validation, peer review, operational readiness, or decision-system status.

## Reviewer request

A reviewer is asked to evaluate whether the framework is methodologically clear, whether assumptions are correctly bounded, whether empirical diagnostics are appropriately described, and whether the limitations are sufficient for a descriptive-only release.
